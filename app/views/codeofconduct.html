Code of Conduct

DADACONF is dedicated to providing a harassment-free conference experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, age or religion, ability to code, level of weird or otherwise. We do not tolerate harassment of conference participants in any form. Conference participants violating these rules may be sanctioned or expelled from the conference without a refund at the discretion of the conference organizers (Siegfried Bilstein, Joey Yang, Christine Cooper).

Harassment includes verbal comments that reinforce social structures of domination related to gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, age, religion; sexual images not used as part of art or projects at DADACONF; deliberate intimidation; stalking; following; harassing photography or recording; sustained disruption of talks or other events; inappropriate physical contact; and unwelcome sexual attention. Participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately.

If a participant engages in harassing behavior, the conference organizers may take any action they deem appropriate, including warning the offender or expulsion from the conference with no refund. If you are being harassed, notice that someone else is being harassed, or have any other concerns, please contact a member of conference staff immediately. Get in touch with Joey Yang, Christine Cooper, or Siegfried Bilstein. Conference staff will be happy to help participants contact hotel/venue security or local law enforcement, provide escorts, or otherwise assist those experiencing harassment to feel safe for the duration of the conference. We value your attendance.

We will kick you out if you do anything to make someone feel unsafe or do anything that violates their privacy, safety, or anything else someone does not consent to. This is important to us. This is an art event so we are open to all kinds of experimental forms of expression. Creativity is absolutely encouraged but that does not make harassment of any kind acceptable. If you feel as if this policy limits your expression, get in touch with an organizer and we will figure out what we can do. In order to preserve freedom of expression, we need to exercise responsibility.

If you need assistance or have questions about code of conduct, please reach out to us:

  • Siegfried Bilstein: siggy at dadaconf .com
  • Joey Yang: joey at dadaconf .com
  • Christine Cooper: christine at dadaconf .com
As we get closer to the event, we will be updating this space with more contact information for who you can reach out to for assistance.

We expect participants to follow these rules at all conference venues and conference-related social events.
